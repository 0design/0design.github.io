PMO India Mobile App
10 May 2016

Gyan Lakhwani

10 May 2016

Designer by day, Ninja by night.

The Prime Ministers Office mobile app was a one of a kind initiative by the Government of India and Google. It is the first offical app for a head of state and its aim was to provide a window to the highest office in the country of India. It begun as a contest held by Google and MyGov( the government of Indias online citizen participation tool ) which saw more than 45,000 ideas sourced and more than 10,000 entries, after which we and our team, were chosen to create the app.

First Design

Our first design strictly adhered to Googles material design guidelines,as Google required us to do so, and all interactions,animations and illustrations were made in strict accordance to them. Gyan led the design team and we worked with assistance from the UI/UX Head of Google India as well as several senior software engineers. We also had a one day session at Google Hyderabad where we were made of several best practices which we should follow during the design and development of the app. One of our primary challenges was to design a style which maintains the stature of the PMOs office while also having an inclusive and warm feel.Apart from this ,given that the app would be used by such a wide audience, accessibility was also something which we gave a lot of importance, as we followed the various industry standards as well as doing a little bit extra wherever we could. After several months of work and a combination of the preceding factors we finally designed and implemented our first design.

Second Design

Following the completion of the design and development it was decided that a few superficial changes were needed to be made in the design style so as to differentiate it from a standard material while retaining the core elements from the previous design. We worked on the redesign with assistance from Creativeland Asia ,a design agency , and also went to work with them for a week at their head office in Mumbai. One of the big changes in the redesign was the use of the font Ek Mukta instead of Roboto. This was because Ek Mukta assured uniformity across Hindi and English which resulted in a better visual feel. Finally 10 languages were integrated into the app. Once completed the changes were implemented and this is the design present in the current Prime Ministers Office app.
